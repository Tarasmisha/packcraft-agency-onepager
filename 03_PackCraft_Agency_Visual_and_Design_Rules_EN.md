# PackCraft for Agencies — Visual and Design Rules Source

## Purpose
This file contains the approved design logic and visual constraints for generating a design-ready one-pager blueprint.

Use this file when NotebookLM is asked to produce:
- visual direction
- art direction requirements
- layout guidance
- design assembly instructions

---

## 1. Design Intent
The one-pager should feel:
- clean
- premium
- commercially serious
- operator-led
- calm and confident

It should not feel:
- playful
- startup-hype
- crowded
- futuristic
- decorative

The visual tone should support trust, clarity, and usability.

---

## 2. Core Design System
### Color logic
Neutral structure:
- primary text / dark elements: #0B0B0C
- secondary text: #6B7280
- background: #FAFAFA
- surface: #FFFFFF
- border: #E5E7EB

Action accent only:
- accent: #EA580C
- accent hover: #DC4F0A
- accent light: #FFF7ED

Important rule:
Do NOT use orange as body text or heading color.
Orange is for action, links, buttons, focus, or small accent use.

---

## 3. Typography
### Heading font
Plus Jakarta Sans
- headings only
- H1 to H4
- weight 600–700

### Body font
Inter
- body text
- buttons
- labels
- UI copy

Important rule:
Do NOT use Inter for headings.
Do NOT use Jakarta for body copy.

---

## 4. Layout Rules
- max content width: 1200px
- equal side margins
- strong alignment
- generous whitespace
- clean section breaks
- equal-height cards when cards appear in a row
- one dominant CTA at a time

The one-pager should not look busy.
It should read like a premium operator document, not a feature dump.

---

## 5. Best Format for This Asset
Recommended final format:
- A4
- 2-page PDF
- portrait

This allows:
- page 1 = commercial case
- page 2 = objections, pilot, proof, CTA

If adapted later to web:
- keep same structure
- keep one dominant CTA above the fold
- stack comparison/timeline blocks vertically on mobile

---

## 6. Recommended Visual Flow
Page 1:
- Hero
- Margin Math
- What You Actually Get
- Production Pack in Action

Page 2:
- Why This Is Not Just Claude + Prompts
- White-Label and Control
- How the Free Pilot Works
- Founder Proof
- Pricing + CTA

Reason:
Page 1 must prove business value.
Page 2 must remove risk and close action.

---

## 7. Block-Level Visual Guidance
### Hero
Best format:
- split layout
- headline and CTA on the left
- compact economics visual on the right

Show:
- big margin number
- simple commercial framing

Do NOT show:
- robots
- abstract AI art
- generic office stock photos

### Margin Math
Best format:
- clean comparison table
- before / with PackCraft logic
- large numbers and restrained labels

Show:
- retainer amount
- production cost
- gross margin
- 5-client scenario if space allows

Do NOT show:
- dense spreadsheets
- small unreadable numbers

### What You Actually Get
Best format:
- four equal-height cards or one structured pack diagram

Show:
- copy
- designer brief
- video script
- checklist

Do NOT show:
- agent counts
- technical architecture clutter

### Production Pack in Action
Best format:
- mini workflow strip or role-based sequence

Show:
- SMM
- designer
- videographer
- manager
all using one shared pack

Do NOT show:
- full unreadable screenshots of long documents

### Why Not Claude + Prompts
Best format:
- side-by-side comparison card

Show:
- text generation vs production architecture
- role-based output
- repeatable workflow
- review flow

Do NOT show:
- emotional competitor attacks
- logo wars

### White-Label and Control
Best format:
- reassurance block with 3 short columns or trust cards

Show:
- your brand
- your final review
- your client relationship

### Pilot Flow
Best format:
- 4–5 step timeline
- numbered circles
- short labels

Show:
- one real client
- free pilot
- review
- scale if it works

### Founder Proof
Best format:
- compact founder note
- short credibility line
- one small portrait if available

Do NOT show:
- long biography
- large personal story block

### Pricing + CTA
Best format:
- one compact pricing strip
- one dominant CTA button
- minimal extra copy

Show:
- free pilot on first client
- $800/client/month
- setup logic for additional clients

---

## 8. Reading Rhythm
The page should move like this:
1. business impact
2. operational clarity
3. proof of usefulness
4. objection removal
5. safe next step

Do not create a page that feels like:
- “features first”
- “brand story first”
- “AI explanation first”

---

## 9. What the first screen must accomplish
The first screen must answer:
- who this is for
- what changes commercially
- what the offer is
- what the next step is

If the buyer does not understand those four things fast, the hero failed.

---

## 10. What the final CTA area must accomplish
The last section must:
- restate the commercial model simply
- reduce hesitation
- make the next step obvious

It should feel low-friction, practical, and reversible:
**Start a free pilot on one client**

---

## 11. Visual mistakes to avoid
Avoid:
- too much copy in one section
- decorative AI visuals
- multiple competing CTAs
- color-heavy layout
- too many icons
- tiny comparison text
- long paragraphs under pricing
- mixed typography hierarchy
- Telegram as the headline concept

Telegram is an operational detail, not the hero.

---

## 12. Helpful design micro-principles
- big numbers create immediate comprehension
- whitespace signals confidence
- short labels reduce reading effort
- equal card heights reduce visual noise
- visual proof should support, not overwhelm, the copy
- every block should make one argument only
